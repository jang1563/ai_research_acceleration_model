### 1.2 Calibration Targets

For each shift, we calibrate against three metrics:
- **Time Acceleration**: How much faster processes became (log scale)
- **Publication Increase**: Field growth in publications/year (log scale)
- **Transformation Years**: Time to reach 10x impact

### 1.3 Mathematical Approach

**Bayesian Parameter Estimation:**

```
P(θ|D) ∝ P(D|θ) × P(θ)
```

Where:
- θ = Model parameters (capability scale, infrastructure boost, etc.)
- D = Historical observations
- P(D|θ) = Gaussian likelihood with heteroscedastic errors
- P(θ) = Informative priors from literature

## 2. Key Insights from Historical Analysis

### 2.1 Shift Categories Matter

**Capability Extensions** (microscope, telescope, HGP, sequencing, X-ray crystallography, cryo-EM):
- Enable new observations or measurements
- High acceleration potential (10-4,700x)
- Impact depends on infrastructure and data accessibility

**Methodological Shifts** (CRISPR, recombinant DNA, PCR):
- Change how research is conducted
- Wide range of acceleration (15x to 1,000,000x for PCR)
- Impact amplified by democratization

**Computational Shifts** (GenBank, BLAST, Rosetta, ML) - NEW in v0.2.1:
- Transform data analysis and prediction capabilities
- Cumulative effects: each generation builds on previous (~10x per step)
- Critical for understanding AI as continuation vs. discontinuity

**Conceptual Paradigm Shifts** (germ theory, central dogma):
- Change fundamental assumptions
- Enabled by capability extensions but require human insight
- **Not directly predictable** from technology capabilities

### 2.2 Amplification Factors

**Infrastructure Investment:**
- HGP required $3B upfront investment
- Enabled 4,700x time acceleration, 15,000,000x cost reduction
- **Key insight:** Front-loaded investment unlocks exponential returns

**Technology Democratization:**
- CRISPR reduced gene editing from expert-only to routine
- DNA sequencing went from core facilities to benchtop
- **Key insight:** Accessibility drives adoption and secondary innovations

### 2.3 Historical Trajectory Patterns

```
Initial Breakthrough → Technology Improvement → Democratization → Field Explosion → Secondary Breakthroughs
```

This pattern appears consistently across:
- Sequencing: Sanger → Illumina → Nanopore → Single-cell genomics
- Gene editing: ZFN → TALEN → CRISPR → Base editing → Prime editing
- Imaging: Light microscopy → Electron microscopy → Cryo-EM → AI-enhanced imaging

### 2.4 Cumulative Computational Acceleration (Key v0.2.1 Insight)

The computational biology expert reviewer (H5) identified a critical pattern for contextualizing AI:

```
Manual → Databases → BLAST → ML → Deep Learning → AI
  1x      ~100x      ~1000x   ~10x    ~10x        ~10x (projected)
```

**Key Implications:**

1. **AI is not a discontinuity** - It represents another ~10x step in a 50-year computational trajectory
2. **Baseline matters** - AlphaFold achieved ~10x over Rosetta (not 1000x over nothing)
3. **Cumulative gains compound** - Total computational acceleration from 1982-2025: ~10^7x
4. **Context for predictions** - The v0.1 model's 38x projection for cognitive stages is consistent with "databases+BLAST+ML+DL" combined effect

This insight fundamentally reframes how we should measure and report AI acceleration - always relative to the computational baseline that preceded it.

## 4. Implications for AI Research Acceleration

### 4.1 Where AI Aligns with Historical Patterns

**Capability Extension (like HGP, sequencing):**
- AI enhances literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, data analysis
- Similar to how sequencing enabled genome-wide studies
- **Expected acceleration:** 100x in cognitive stages (bounded by M_max)

**Democratization (like CRISPR):**
- AI tools becoming accessible to all researchers (ChatGPT, AlphaFold)
- Similar to CRISPR making gene editing accessible
- **Expected multiplier:** 2-3x additional from democratization

### 4.2 Where AI Differs from Historical Precedents

**Physical World Constraints:**
- HGP accelerated sequencing (data generation → becomes cognitive over time)
- AI cannot accelerate cell division, animal growth, clinical trials
- **Critical bottleneck:** S4 (Wet Lab) and S6 (Validation) remain at 2.5x maximum

**The Unlock Potential:**
- If AI creates validated simulation substitutes (like AlphaFold for X-ray crystallography)
- Physical constraints could be bypassed entirely
- **With unlock:** 38x → 100x+ acceleration possible (see v0.1 report)

### 4.3 Calibrated Projections

Based on historical calibration:

| Scenario | 2050 Acceleration | Historical Analog |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Conservative | 15-20x | Methodological shift (CRISPR-like) |
| Baseline | 30-40x | Constrained capability extension |
| With Unlock | 80-150x | Unconstrained capability extension (HGP-like) |
