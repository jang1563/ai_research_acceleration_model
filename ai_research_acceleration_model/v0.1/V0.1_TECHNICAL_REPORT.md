## 1. Model Architecture

### 1.1 Theoretical Foundation

The model is grounded in Dario Amodei's "Machines of Loving Grace" framework [1], which identifies five limiting factors on AI-driven scientific acceleration:

1. **Speed of Physical World** - Cell growth, hardware build time
2. **Data Scarcity** - Training data limits
3. **Intrinsic Complexity** - Chaos theory, unpredictability
4. **Human Constraints** - Laws, regulations, habits
5. **Physical Laws** - Absolute constraints

Our model quantifies how these constraints create a "funnel" that limits the translation of raw AI intelligence into scientific output.

### 1.2 The 8-Stage Research Pipeline

| ID | Stage | Duration | Type | M_max Speed | M_max Quality |
|----|-------|----------|------|-------------|---------------|
| S1 | Literature Synthesis | 3 mo | Cognitive | 100x | 30x |
| S2 | Hypothesis Generation | 6 mo | Cognitive | 100x | 20x |
| S3 | Experimental Design | 2 mo | Cognitive | 30x | 20x |
| **S4** | **Data Generation (Wet Lab)** | **12 mo** | **Physical** | **2.5x** | **2.5x** |
| S5 | Data Analysis | 3.5 mo | Cognitive | 100x | 50x |
| **S6** | **Validation & Replication** | **8 mo** | **Hybrid** | **2.5x** | **2.5x** |
| S7 | Writing & Peer Review | 6 mo | Social | 10x | 5x |
| S8 | Publication & Dissemination | 3 mo | Social | 20x | 10x |

**Note:** Highlighted rows (S4, S6) indicate physical-world bottlenecks with limited AI acceleration potential.

### 1.3 Mathematical Framework

The core acceleration equation:

```
M_constrained = 1 + (M_raw - 1) × C(t) × Q(t)
```

Where:
- `M_raw` = Raw AI multiplier including PSM effects
- `C(t)` = Infrastructure constraint factor (compute, data, talent)
- `Q(t)` = Quality factor from AI failure modes

System throughput follows Little's Law [2]:
```
Θ(t) = min_i μ_i(t)
```

## 2. The Unlock: Simulation Replacing Physical Trials

A key innovation in v0.1 is modeling the potential for AI to invent simulation tools that replace physical experiments entirely.

### 2.1 Concept

The "Unlock" represents a bypass pathway around physical-world bottlenecks—not just speeding up experiments, but circumventing them through validated in-silico substitutes.

### 2.2 Current Examples

| Domain | Physical Process | AI Simulation Substitute | Status |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------------|--------|
| Protein Structure | X-ray crystallography (months-years) | AlphaFold prediction (minutes) [3] | Deployed |
| Drug Binding | Wet lab binding assays | Molecular dynamics + ML [4] | Emerging |
| Cell Behavior | Cell culture experiments | Virtual cell models [5] | Early |
| Toxicology | Animal testing | In-silico ADMET prediction [6] | Partial |

### 2.3 Unlock Probability Trajectory

| Year | P(Unlock) | Milestone |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| 2025 | 13% | Early validation of concept |
| 2030 | 18% | First regulatory acceptance of in-silico evidence |
| 2040 | 32% | Partial replacement of some experiment types |
| 2050 | 47% | Substantial replacement for validated domains |

### 2.4 Impact on Acceleration

| Scenario | 2050 M_max (Wet Lab) | System Acceleration |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| No Unlock | 2.5x | ~20x |

## 6. Conclusions

### 6.1 Key Findings

1. **Physical-world constraints are binding:** Wet lab data generation (S4) and validation (S6) remain bottlenecks throughout all scenarios, limiting acceleration to ~20x without simulation unlock.

2. **The Unlock pathway matters:** If AI successfully creates validated simulation substitutes for physical experiments, acceleration could reach 38x by 2050 (1.8x additional boost).

3. **Capability extension is most influential:** The PSM Type III weight accounts for 38% of output variance, making it the dominant uncertain parameter.

4. **Scenario range is narrower than expected:** Even with very different AI growth rates (g=0.15 to 0.55), 2050 acceleration only varies from 15x to 22x due to physical constraints.
