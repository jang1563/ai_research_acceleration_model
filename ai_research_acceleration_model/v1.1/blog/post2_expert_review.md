## Unanimous Strengths

All four experts praised:

1. **The Pizza Delivery Analogy** - "Every teenager has impatiently tracked a delivery" - immediately graspable
2. **The "Year 4225" Materials Science Stat** - "Genuinely jaw-dropping" and highly shareable
3. **The Bits/Hybrid/Atoms Framework** - "Gives students a mental tool they can apply independently"
4. **Respects Student Intelligence** - Uses real data without condescending

---

## Consensus Weaknesses

### 1. Too Long (All Experts)
- 15 minutes exceeds teen attention span
- **Fix**: Cut factory analogy (redundant), add TL;DR after first 3 analogies

### 2. Missing Emotional Hook (HS Teacher, SciComm)
- "Intellectually engaging but emotionally neutral"
- **Fix**: Open with specific patient story (Parkinson's grandmother, ALS patient)

### 3. Outdated References (Gen-Z)
- "Plot twist" peaked ~2019
- "Unskippable cutscenes" is PS3-era
- Zero mention of ChatGPT, AI art controversy, job anxiety
- **Fix**: Update references, acknowledge AI anxiety

### 4. Insufficient Assessment (Instructional Designer)
- Only 3 questions at the end
- No explicit learning objectives
- **Fix**: Add "Check Your Understanding" boxes every 5-7 minutes

### 5. Career Section Too Vague (HS Teacher)
- "Consider lab automation engineering" - a 16-year-old can't act on this
- **Fix**: Add concrete next steps: "Search YouTube for X, take AP Chemistry, look up iGEM teams"

## Gen-Z Rewrite Example

The Gen-Z Strategist provided this rewrite of the Big Picture section:

> **The TL;DR (No Because Actually Read This)**
>
> Look. Here is what is actually going on:
>
> **AI is cracked at the computer stuff.** Predictions that used to take years? Minutes now. That part is real. That part is insane.
>
> **But science is not just vibes and algorithms.** It is also growing actual cells (boring), running experiments (expensive), testing drugs on real humans (takes literal years), and manufacturing things in the physical world (even more boring). Physics does not care about your GPU.
>
> **The "pipeline discount" is why the headlines are lying to you.** When someone says "AI is 1000x faster," they mean ONE step got faster. The whole process? Maybe 2x faster. Maybe less. It is giving "we have food at home" energy.
>
> **2.8x faster is still kind of unreal though.** That means scientific progress that would have taken 100 years now takes 35. Treatments that were supposed to be for your grandkids? Might be for you.
>
> **The bottleneck is not the AI anymore. It is everything else.**
>
> Could not be you? Could be you though.
