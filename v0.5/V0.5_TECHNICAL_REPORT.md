## 1. Motivation

### 1.1 The Physical Bottleneck Problem

v0.4 validated that physical stages limit end-to-end acceleration:

```
Cognitive stages (S1, S2, S3, S5): 60-500x acceleration
Physical stages (S4, S6):          1.0-1.5x acceleration
End-to-end:                        1.6-2.4x acceleration
```

The vast gains in computational stages are "wasted" because physical validation cannot keep pace.

### 1.2 Autonomous Labs as the Solution

Autonomous laboratories can dramatically accelerate physical stages:

- **A-Lab (Berkeley)**: 350 materials/year vs 50-100 manual
- **High-throughput screening**: 100,000 compounds/day vs 100 manual
- **Cloud labs (Emerald, Strateos)**: 24/7 operation vs 8-hour shifts

If physical stages can be accelerated to 5-50x, the end-to-end bottleneck shifts back to cognitive stages, and total acceleration can exceed 10x.

### 2.4 Integrated Model

The v0.5 integrated model combines:

1. **v0.4 cognitive acceleration** (domain-specific, shift-type aware)
2. **v0.5 physical acceleration** (lab automation)
3. **Cost dynamics** (learning curve cost reductions)

```python
class IntegratedAccelerationModel:
    def stage_acceleration(self, stage_id, year):
        if is_cognitive:
            return cognitive_model.stage_acceleration(stage_id, year)
        else:
            return lab_model.physical_acceleration(year)[stage_id]
```

## 5. Key Insights

### 5.1 Physical Bottleneck is Unlockable

The v0.4 conclusion that "physical stages limit everything to 2-3x" is **conditional on manual labs**. With automation:

- Physical stage M_max increases from 1.5x to 5-50x
- End-to-end acceleration can reach 10-40x
- The bottleneck shifts back to adoption and integration

### 5.2 Domain Heterogeneity Persists

Even with automation, domains differ significantly:

- **Structural biology** benefits most (AlphaFold already reduced physical needs)
- **Materials science** sees moderate gains (synthesis remains rate-limiting)
- **Drug discovery** gains are limited by clinical trials (not automatable)

### 5.3 Investment Implications

The scenario matrix shows that:

1. **AI investment alone** yields 2-3x (v0.4 projection)
2. **Automation investment alone** yields 2-4x
3. **Combined investment** yields 6-10x (multiplicative gains)

**Recommendation:** R&D portfolios should balance AI and automation investments.

## 8. Limitations

### 8.1 Lab Deployment Assumptions

The model assumes labs can be deployed at scale. Factors not modeled:

- Capital costs of building automated labs
- Training and staffing requirements
- Regulatory approval for automated systems
- Supply chain for lab materials

### 8.2 Integration Challenges

Automated labs need integration with AI systems:

- Data pipelines from lab to model
- Closed-loop experiment design
- Quality control and error handling

### 8.3 Domain-Specific Barriers

Some physical processes may resist automation:

- Clinical trials (regulatory, ethical)
- Field research (ecology, environment)
- Human subject research
